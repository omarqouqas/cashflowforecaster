**Research Method:** Web search across Reddit communities, forums, and aggregated freelancer resources

## Common Payment Problems (from Reddit)

### 1. Client Ghosting
The most discussed issue on Reddit freelance communities. Freelancers complete work, deliver it, then face silence - clients stop responding to emails, messages, and calls for weeks or months despite having already received the finished product.

### 2. Scope Creep Without Compensation
Clients gradually add "just one more thing" until the project doubles in size, yet refuse to pay additional fees. When freelancers mention extra charges, clients claim additions were "obviously included" in the original agreement.

### 3. Platform Payment Holds and Disputes
Users on Upwork, Fiverr, and Freelancer.com report:
- Clients initiating disputes at the last minute to avoid payment
- Funds held for 14+ days after work acceptance
- Payment processor fees significantly reducing earnings
- Account suspensions that freeze all pending payments

### 4. "Exposure" and False Promises
New freelancers fall for requests to work for exposure or promises of future lucrative projects that never materialize, resulting in unpaid or heavily discounted work.

## What Freelancers Currently Do (Workarounds)

1. **Build Emergency Funds** - Recommended 3-6 months of expenses, but many can't achieve this
2. **Pay Themselves a Salary** - Create artificial stability from variable income
3. **Use Spreadsheets** - Many prefer Google Sheets over complex apps
4. **Require Upfront Deposits** - 25-50% before starting work
5. **Create Two Budgets** - One for lean months, one for high-income months
